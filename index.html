Who is Sachin Sir?

Biography & Personal Profile

Everything you need to know about Sachin Jakhar — his background, personal life, assets, and the story behind the "Village IITian" persona.

Sachin Jakhar is one of the most recognizable Mathematics educators in India's competitive exam preparation ecosystem. He serves as a Lead JEE Mathematics Faculty at Physics Wallah (PW), primarily coaching aspirants targeting JEE Main and JEE Advanced.

Born into a rural background, Sachin Jakhar's rise from a village to the hallways of IIT Delhi is one of the most cited motivational stories among JEE aspirants. This journey forms the core of his "Village IITian" identity — a brand construct that deeply resonates with lakhs of students who come from non-metropolitan towns and villages.

He attended Bansal Classes in Kota as a student, where he experienced the competitive batch reshuffling system first-hand — eventually rising to the elite "P" batch. This experience now directly informs his mentorship style, as he can empathize with the psychological pressures aspirants face.

Sachin Sir is married and frequently incorporates humorous anecdotes from his domestic life into his lectures — including the famous "onion-cutting" story with his wife — creating a warm, relatable classroom environment within an otherwise high-pressure academic setting.

He is known among his colleagues and students for his interest in luxury vehicles, particularly his Land Rover Defender, which frequently appears in personal vlogs shared alongside fellow PW faculty like Rajwant Singh.

Teaching Methodology

What Makes Sachin Sir's Teaching Unique?

Sachin Jakhar's pedagogy blends deep subject expertise with emotional intelligence, creating a distinctive teaching environment.

01
Exhaustive Illustrations

Covers each concept through a large number of solved examples in-class — ensuring students encounter all possible problem variations before the exam. Especially effective for chapters like Trigonometry and Complex Numbers.

02
Zero-to-Hero Approach

Begins every topic from the absolute basics, making his batches ideal for students who have weak or no prior exposure to a topic. No student is left behind due to missing prerequisites.

03
Long-Form Sessions

Classes regularly extend to 3 hours to ensure exhaustive problem coverage. While this demands high stamina from students, it significantly reduces exposure to "new" problem types during actual exams.

04
"Baatcheet" Mentorship

Integrates regular conversational segments into sessions to address backlog management, psychological resilience, and daily challenges of JEE preparation — building a genuine mentor-student bond.

05
Kota Stories & Relatability

Shares his own IIT and Kota experiences including batch reshuffling and setbacks, creating a deeply relatable atmosphere, especially for students from small towns and villages.

06
DPP with Video Solutions

Daily Practice Problems are supplemented with video solutions personally recorded or supervised by him, maintaining pedagogical consistency throughout the course lifecycle.
